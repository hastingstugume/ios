# Niche Growth Strategy

## Decision

Primary niche: **Local SEO/reputation agencies and solo consultants serving home-services SMBs** (plumbers, HVAC, electricians, roofers, cleaners).

This is the fastest path to paid adoption because the buyer pain is immediate, measurable, and easy to tie to revenue outcomes (calls, bookings, quoted jobs).

## Why This Niche First

- Pain is obvious: owners care about calls and booked jobs now, not vanity metrics.
- Services are repeatable: GBP optimization, review response systems, local landing pages, citations, and conversion fixes.
- Delivery can be standardized: agencies can run the same playbook across many local clients.
- Signals are discoverable: local-intent pain appears in public channels and listing ecosystems.

## What We Copy (and Avoid) From Leads-Style Products

### Copy

- Lead + audit + outreach in one flow.
- “Prequalified pain” scoring so users focus on high-probability prospects.
- Fast time-to-first-value (minutes, not hours).
- Verticalized templates (query packs, outreach templates, objections, next steps).

### Avoid

- Opaque data quality claims.
- Setup friction hidden behind a marketing promise.
- Generic templates that feel spammy and reduce trust.
- Weak support/onboarding loops that increase churn.

## Product Wedge

1. **Niche Source Packs**
- Home-services visibility rescue.
- Google Business Profile rescue and review pressure.
- Owner-led recommendation and "need help" demand.

2. **Niche Scoring Layer**
- Boost: explicit urgency, local intent, owner/operator context, recurring revenue potential.
- Penalize: job posts, tutorial chatter, non-buyer educational noise.

3. **Niche Conversion Assets**
- AI summaries framed as: "lost calls", "trust leak", "ranking decline", "bookings at risk".
- One-click first outreach that sounds consultative, not mass cold outreach.

4. **Niche Success Metric Stack**
- Activation: first qualified signal in first 24 hours.
- Value: first outreach-ready signal in first week.
- ROI: first positive reply/meeting in first 30 days.

## Path Toward 1M Subscribers

A single B2B niche is unlikely to reach 1M **paying** users. Treat 1M as a **registered-user** milestone with expansion:

1. Win local home-services agencies.
2. Expand to adjacent local verticals (dental, legal, medspa, real estate services).
3. Launch SMB-owner self-serve motion with guided playbooks.
4. Layer channel partnerships, affiliates, and white-label distribution.

## 12-Month Execution Shape

### Phase 1 (0-90 days)
- Dominate one narrow niche workflow.
- Ship niche templates + scoring + outreach assets.
- Build proof loops (case studies, before/after outcomes).

### Phase 2 (90-180 days)
- Add two adjacent vertical packs.
- Introduce stronger onboarding recommendations based on account type and keywords.
- Add conversion analytics by source/template.

### Phase 3 (180-365 days)
- Expand self-serve distribution.
- Add partner/affiliate surfaces.
- Add multi-vertical packaging and pricing.

## Non-Negotiables

- No fake lead claims.
- Every score must be explainable.
- Every upgrade promise must map to a real capability.
- Every new source must pass policy/compliance guardrails.

## External Data Points (Market Reality)

- U.S. small businesses remain a very large market (SBA/related reporting).
- SMEs dominate firm counts globally in OECD and World Bank reporting.
- Google Maps includes a massive business/place index, reinforcing local-business data opportunity.
